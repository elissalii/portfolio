NOAA GLANSIS

a one stop shop for all your Great Lakes Species Information

overview

The GLANSIS website plays a crucial role in fortifying Great Lakes protection by monitoring nonindigenous species. However, its current design lacks accessibility, usability, and modernity, constraining the organization's overall impact. Acknowledging GLANSIS' significance, the redesign focuses on streamlining the Species Generator List for enhanced accessibility, incorporating responsive design for inclusivity. By simplifying navigation and content, the redesign empowers a broader audience to understand GLANSIS' importance in Great Lakes conservation.

01 - the problem

The pervasive threat of invasive species in the Great Lakes has led to dire consequences, including the mass endangerment of native species and a substantial decline in local fisheries' revenue. According to NOAA, a staggering 41% of these non-native species are inflicting considerable harm. Despite extensive efforts and expenditures exceeding $200 million on preventative measures and mitigating income losses, the problem persists as a severe and longstanding menace to the region.

A critical aspect exacerbating this issue is the outdated and inefficient design of the GLANSIS website, specifically the Species List Generator, the primary platform for monitoring nonindigenous species. The current lack of accessibility, usability, and modernity hinders the organization's ability to effectively communicate and engage stakeholders in the fight against invasive species. Addressing this design shortfall is paramount in fortifying GLANSIS' impact and, consequently, the preservation of the Great Lakes ecosystem.
